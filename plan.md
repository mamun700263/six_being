# THE LAST SIX

---

## OPENING

The world ended 47 days ago.

Not with a bang. Not with a warning. Just a virus that spread faster than the news could report it. Now the streets are empty, the power flickers, and the dead don't stay dead.

You survived alone for 47 days. Eating scraps. Hiding in shadows. Slowly losing your mind from the silence.

Then you heard a voice.

---

## ACT 1 — THE STRANGER

A figure appears at your shelter door. Hands up. No weapons visible.

```
 _____________________
|                     |
|   "Hey. Don't       |
|    shoot. I'm       |
|    one of the       |
|    good ones."      |
|_____________________|
        |
       \|/
   ( •  • )
    \  ▽  /
     |    |
    _|    |_
```

His name is **Kai.**

He's warm. Funny. Disarming. Exactly the kind of person you want to trust in the apocalypse.

He tells you about his group. Five people. Smart, capable, survivors. But they got separated during a raid three days ago. Scattered across the city. He's been searching for them ever since but the zones are too dangerous to enter alone.

He needs your help.

*"Help me find them,"* he says. *"And I promise — you won't be alone anymore."*

You don't know yet that Kai is the one who caused the separation. That he made a deal with another faction to save himself. That the guilt is eating him alive. That he chose you specifically because you looked desperate enough to say yes.

But you say yes.

Because 47 days of silence will make you trust anyone.

---

## THE FIVE — WHO YOU'RE SEARCHING FOR

Before you leave, Kai tells you about each of them:

---

**ALEX — "The Soldier"**
```
  |  |
 [    ]
 |    |
  \  /
   \/
  ALEX
```
*"Alex is our fighter. Ex-military. Saved all of us at least once. Just... don't question his decisions in front of people. He doesn't take that well."*

What Kai doesn't tell you: Alex has started rationing supplies in his favour. He believes leaders deserve more. He believes he is the leader.

---

**MAYA — "The Brain"**
```
  ___
 /   \
| ◉ ◉ |
|  ▿  |
 \___/
  MAYA
```
*"Maya is the smartest person I've ever met. Puzzles, codes, patterns — she sees things nobody else does. Just let her think she's in charge and she'll do anything for you."*

What Kai doesn't tell you: Maya already calculated that the group has a 34% survival chance with six people and a 61% chance with three. She is quietly deciding who is worth keeping.

```

  Jordan
  😂😂
```
*"Jordan keeps everyone sane. Always joking, always laughing. In the darkest moments Jordan will make you smile. You need that out here."*

What Kai doesn't tell you: Jordan laughs because the alternative is breaking down completely. Two weeks ago Jordan froze during a zombie encounter and someone almost died. Nobody has talked about it since. Jordan uses humour to bury the guilt.

---

**SAM — "The Ghost"**

*"Sam is quiet. Keeps to himself. But he notices everything — numbers, patterns, who has what. Invaluable for resource management."*

Riley will be watching you from the moment you meet.

## THE FIVE ZONES — WHERE THEY'RE TRAPPED

Each friend is trapped in a different location across the city. Each zone has its own danger, its own puzzle, and its own moral test.

---

**ZONE 1 — THE HOSPITAL**
*Alex is here.*

A crumbling hospital overrun with zombies. Medical equipment everywhere. The power is on backup generator — flickering, unstable.

```
  _______________________
 |   ST. MERCY HOSPITAL  |
 |_______________________|
 |  |  |        |  |  |  |
 |  |  |  ZMBZ  |  |  |  |
 |__|__|________|__|__|__|
      ☣️  DANGER  ☣️
```

**The Puzzle:** A locked door with a medical code. Maya could solve it in seconds but she's not here. You have to figure it out yourself — or convince Alex through the door to guide you, but his ego means he'll only help if you ask the right way.

**The Zombie Encounter:** A horde in the corridor. Alex can fight through but only if he has enough HP. If his HP is low because of earlier choices, he might not make it out alone.

**The Moral Test:** You find a medical supply cache. Enough life points for the whole group. Do you report the full amount to Kai, or pocket some before telling anyone?

---

**ZONE 2 — THE UNIVERSITY LIBRARY**
*Maya is here.*

Quiet. Eerie. Books everywhere. Maya has barricaded herself in the archive room and won't open the door for anyone she doesn't trust.

```
 ___________________________
|   CITY UNIVERSITY LIBRARY |
|___________________________|
| 📚  📚  📚  📚  📚  📚   |
|     [ARCHIVE - LOCKED]    |
|___________________________|
```

**The Puzzle:** Maya won't open the door unless you solve a logic problem she slides under the door. She designed it specifically so only someone genuinely intelligent — or someone she has briefed — could solve it. You're on your own.

**The Zombie Encounter:** While you're solving the puzzle a zombie enters the building. You can run, fight, or use the environment cleverly. If you run, Maya sees you abandon the challenge and trusts you less immediately.

**The Moral Test:** Maya tells you she has been calculating survival odds. She tells you, coldly, that not everyone in the group is going to make it and you should start thinking strategically. She offers to share her calculations — but only if you agree to make decisions *her* way going forward. Do you agree?

---

**ZONE 3 — THE SHOPPING CENTRE**
*Jordan is here.*

Surprisingly well stocked. Jordan has been surviving comfortably here and has found significant resources — food, medicine, life points. But Jordan has also been alone for three days and is starting to unravel.

```
 ___________________________
|    WESTFIELD SURVIVAL     |
|___________________________|
| 🏪 | 💊 | 🍕 | 🔫 | 🏪  |
|    |    |    |    |     |
|___________________________|
  ⚠️ ZOMBIE PACK — LEVEL 3
```

**The Puzzle:** Jordan has rigged the entrance with traps — panicked, improvised, dangerous. You have to talk Jordan down through a radio you find at the entrance. This is a wordplay challenge — saying the right things to someone on the edge.

**The Zombie Encounter:** The largest horde so far. Jordan has supplies but froze last time. If you don't handle this carefully Jordan freezes again and you both take heavy damage.

**The Moral Test:** Jordan found significantly more resources than needed and has been using them freely — wasting some. When you arrive the total is less than it should have been. Jordan is embarrassed. Do you shame Jordan publicly when you reunite with the group, stay quiet, or use it as leverage later?

---

**ZONE 4 — THE UNDERGROUND STATION**
*Sam is here.*

Dark. Cold. The perfect place for someone who doesn't want to be found. Sam chose this location deliberately. Hard to reach, defensible, and full of abandoned supplies that Sam has already catalogued and partially redistributed — to himself.

```
 ___________________________
|   ⬇️  UNDERGROUND  ⬇️    |
|___________________________|
|  [LINE 1]    [LINE 2]     |
|  =========  =========    |
|   PLATFORM    DARK        |
|___________________________|
    🧟 🧟 🧟 ROAMING
```

**The Puzzle:** Sam has encrypted his location with a number sequence. He broadcasts it on loop on a radio frequency — but only someone who understands the mathematical pattern can decode it. This is the hardest puzzle in the game.

**The Zombie Encounter:** Fast zombies. Multiple directions. Sam will help but calculates his own safety first — he will not put himself at significant risk for you. Noticeably.

**The Moral Test:** Sam shows you his ledger. Every transaction the group has ever made. Including some discrepancies that point toward another group member stealing. He offers you the information — as a bargaining chip. *"You might want to know who you can trust."* But accepting it means you owe Sam something. And Sam always collects.

---

**ZONE 5 — THE RADIO TOWER**
*Riley is here.*

The highest point in the city. Riley climbed here to watch. To see everything. And Riley has seen a lot.

```
        /\
       /  \
      / 📡 \
     /______\
     |      |
     |      |
  ___|      |___
     TOWER
  ⚡ STORM COMING ⚡
```

**The Puzzle:** Riley communicates only in coded messages at first — testing you. Every response you give is being analysed. This is a trust puzzle more than a logic puzzle. There are no right answers, only honest ones and dishonest ones. Riley already knows which is which.

**The Zombie Encounter:** A storm is coming. Zombies are drawn to the tower's emergency light. You have to navigate this together — and Riley will be watching how you perform under pressure when you think nobody important is judging you.

**The Moral Test:** Riley tells you quietly, alone, that they know Kai is hiding something. That the group's separation wasn't an accident. Riley asks you directly — *"Do you trust Kai?"* Your answer here echoes all the way to the ending.

---

## ACT 3 — THE COLLAPSE

All six of you are together now. For one brief moment it feels like safety.

Then the resources start running short faster than they should.

Nobody says anything. But the numbers don't add up. Everyone is looking at everyone else. Trust is fracturing quietly.

Then the final zone appears — the only exit from the city. A bridge. One route out. And a massive horde between you and freedom.

```
 ================================
|     THE BRIDGE — FINAL ZONE    |
 ================================
      🧟🧟🧟🧟🧟🧟🧟🧟
 ________________________________
|__THE ONLY WAY OUT OF THE CITY__|
      
  ⚠️  NOT EVERYONE HAS ENOUGH HP
  ⚠️  SOMEONE NEEDS TO STAY BACK
```

The game forces you here. Someone has to hold the horde off. Someone has to stay.

But who?

---

## THE BETRAYAL PHASE

This is where everything you did comes back.

The game looks at:
- Who you stole from
- Who stole from you
- Who you helped
- Who you covered for
- What you told Riley about Kai
- Whether you took Maya's deal
- Whether you shamed Jordan
- Whether you accepted Sam's ledger

And it forces a brutal sequence of choices. Not clean moral choices. Messy, emotional, complicated ones.

Friends might volunteer to stay — but for the wrong reasons.
Friends might accuse each other in the chaos.
Kai might finally confess.
Riley might reveal everything they know.

And one by one, people don't make it across the bridge.

---

## THE ENDINGS

**Ending 1 — "The Soldier Remains"**
Alex stays back. Dies the way he always wanted — fighting. Called you a good leader at the end. You know you drained him in Zone 1. He never knew. He died proud of you.

**Ending 2 — "The Numbers Made Sense"**
Maya calculated that her survival gave the group the best odds. Then the group made a different calculation. She understood. She said nothing. Just nodded. That was Maya's way.

**Ending 3 — "Still Laughing"**
Jordan made a joke as the horde came. Everyone managed a small smile. Jordan always said if you could laugh you could survive anything. Jordan was wrong. But Jordan wasn't really wrong either.

**Ending 4 — "Even Stevens"**
Sam stayed because the ledger finally balanced. He'd taken enough. He knew it. In the end Sam paid it back the only way left. You find his private notes later. Every number accounted for. Including yours.

**Ending 5 — "I Knew All Along"**
Riley looks at you before the bridge. Not angry. Not sad. Just knowing.
*"I saw everything from that tower. Everything everyone did. I stayed because I chose to. Not because you made me."*
*"Take care of yourself out there. You're better at surviving than you think."*
*"Just... try to be better at living too."*
